# Mundiapolis Library Product Context

register: product

## Product Purpose

Mundiapolis Library is an authenticated university library system for Mundiapolis students, faculty, librarians, and admins. It helps students discover books, request borrows, manage active loans, review returned books, and track account status. It helps admins manage catalog quality, account approvals, borrow requests, renewals, fines, reminders, and operating analytics.

## Users

- Students who need fast catalog search, clear availability, and low-friction borrow requests.
- Library staff who need dense, reliable operational screens for approvals, circulation, and metadata maintenance.
- Admins who need trustworthy dashboards, exports, reminders, and user management without hunting through visual noise.

## Scene

A student checks book availability between classes on a phone in a bright campus hallway, while an admin reviews circulation and account requests on a desktop in a quiet library office. The interface should feel scholarly, calm, and efficient in both settings.

## Brand And Tone

- Scholarly, precise, modern, and institutional.
- Confident but not grandiose.
- Warm Mundiapolis character through restrained navy and gold accents.
- Copy should be direct, helpful, and task-focused.

## Strategic Principles

- Product utility comes first. The design should disappear into discovery, borrowing, and admin work.
- Use familiar product patterns: top navigation, sidebar navigation, tabs, filters, tables, clear forms, and persistent actions.
- Preserve density where admins need it, but keep hierarchy legible.
- Treat availability, request status, overdue state, and admin permissions as first-class information.
- Avoid decorative spectacle that competes with catalog content.

## Anti-References

- Generic dark SaaS dashboards with blue-purple gradients.
- Luxury finance navy-and-gold styling.
- Glassmorphism as default surface treatment.
- Repeated icon-card grids.
- Marketing landing pages that delay the actual library task.
- Academic websites that look archival, static, or bureaucratic.
